Physics Lab · Experiment 05
Energy Bandgap of a Semiconductor
Determine the energy bandgap of a p-n junction diode using temperature-dependent reverse saturation current.
Sections
Progress 20%
🎯
Aim
Objective of this experiment

To determine the energy bandgap (Eg) of a semiconductor material (Germanium or Silicon) by studying the temperature dependence of the reverse saturation current (Is) of a p-n junction diode.

Learning Objective: Understand how the energy band gap of a semiconductor governs its electrical behavior with temperature. By plotting Log₁₀(Is) vs 1000/T, we extract the slope and calculate Eg using the relation Eg = (2 × 2.303 × k × |slope| × 10³) / q.

By the end of this experiment, you will be able to measure reverse saturation current at varying temperatures, construct the observation table, plot the characteristic graph, determine the slope from two best-fit points, and calculate the energy bandgap in electron Volts (eV).

Subject Tags:

Semiconductor Physics p-n Junction Band Theory Boltzmann Equation Thermometry Germanium / Silicon

Description
Theory & Background

In a semiconductor, electrons can exist in two energy bands separated by a forbidden region called the Energy Band Gap (Eg). The valence band is the lower energy band where electrons are normally present, while the conduction band is where electrons move freely and contribute to electrical conduction.

The reverse saturation current (Is) of a p-n junction diode increases exponentially with temperature because more electron-hole pairs are thermally generated as the temperature rises.

Diode Current Equation
I = Is [exp(Eg/kT) − 1]
Band Gap Formula
Eg = (2 × 2.303 × k × |slope| × 10³) / q
Graph Relationship
Y = Log₁₀(Is) vs X = 1000/T

When a graph is drawn with Log₁₀(Is) on the Y-axis and 1000/T on the X-axis, a straight line is obtained. The slope of this best-fit line is related to the energy band gap Eg.

Apparatus Required: p-n junction diode (Ge or Si), variable power supply, microammeter (μA), voltmeter (V), oven for temperature control, connecting wires, thermometer.

Constants: Boltzmann constant k = 1.38 × 10⁻²³ J/K  |  Elementary charge q = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C

Theoretical values:

Germanium: ≈ 0.67 eV
Silicon: ≈ 1.12 eV
GaAs: ≈ 1.42 eV
InP: ≈ 1.35 eV
📝
Procedure
Step-by-step instructions

Follow each step carefully to perform the energy bandgap experiment with proper safety precautions and accurate measurements.

  1. Connect the p-n junction diode in reverse bias configuration with the oven, microammeter (μA), voltmeter (V), and variable power supply as shown in the circuit diagram.
  2. Set the power supply to a fixed reverse voltage (e.g., 5V). Ensure all connections are firm and the voltmeter reads the reverse bias voltage across the diode.
  3. Switch on the oven and begin heating. Record the temperature t (°C) and corresponding reverse saturation current Is (μA) at regular 5°C intervals.
  4. Convert temperature from Celsius to Kelvin using T = t + 273. Calculate 1000/T and Log₁₀(Is) for each reading.
  5. Record at least 8–9 readings for the temperature range 35°C to 75°C. Do not exceed 90°C as it may damage the diode.
  6. Plot a graph of Log₁₀(Is) on the Y-axis vs 1000/T on the X-axis. Draw the best-fit straight line.
  7. Calculate the slope (m) using two widely-separated best-fit points on the line: m = ΔY / ΔX = Δ[Log₁₀(Is)] / Δ[1000/T].
  8. Apply the band gap formula: Eg = (2 × 2.303 × k × |slope| × 10³) / q eV to calculate the energy bandgap of the semiconductor.
⚠️ Precautions:
  1. The current flow should not be too high.
  2. Connections must be made properly.
  3. Temperature of the oven should not exceed 90°C.
  4. Take readings only after the temperature stabilizes.
